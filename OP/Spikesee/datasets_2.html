SPIFT and PHM Datasets

SPIFT (SPIkingly Flying Things) and PHM (Photo-realistic High-speed Motion) datasets were published in the paper Optical Flow Estimation for Spiking Camera.

SPIFT is the first spike-based optical flow estimation training dataset, containing 100 scenes, each describing the translational and rotational motion of different objects against random backgrounds.

Dataset Features

  • Contains 100 diverse scenes
  • Each scene provides 500 frames of spike signal stream
  • Provides corresponding real images and optical flow data
  • Based on a high frame rate spike camera of 40000Hz

Optical Flow Data Generation

  • Generates optical flow data every 10 spike frames
  • Generates optical flow data every 20 spike frames
  • Considering the small motion amplitude at high frame rates, optical flow is not generated for every frame

SSES Dataset

SSES (Spike-based Synthetic validation dataset for Extreme Scenarios in autonomous driving) is the first synthetic dataset focused on extreme scenarios in autonomous driving for spike cameras. This dataset was published in the paper Unsupervised Optical Flow Estimation with Dynamic Timing Representation for Spike Camera.

Dataset Significance

  • Filling the gap: Provides the first synthetic dataset for algorithm validation of spike cameras in extreme scenarios of autonomous driving
  • Promoting unsupervised learning: Supports the training and evaluation of unsupervised optical flow estimation models, reducing dependence on real annotated data
  • Enhancing robustness: Helps models adapt to complex dynamic environments through diverse extreme scenario designs, promoting the development of safety-critical applications

Data Generation Method

  • Constructs diverse scenes based on the CARLA simulator
  • Renders RGB frames at 500fps
  • Increases frame rate to 40kHz through optical flow interpolation
  • Simulates the "integrate-and-fire" mechanism of spike cameras
  • Provides ground truth for optical flow, instance segmentation, and depth information
  • Each data sequence lasts 0.5-1.5 seconds

Scene Characteristics

  • Contains 10 extreme scenarios simulating traffic accidents caused by traffic rule violations or visual blind spots
  • Covers various street environments, background vehicles (speed: 10-16 m/s)
  • Includes pedestrians/cyclists (speed: 5-8 m/s)
  • Supports various weather conditions (rainy, foggy)

Dataset Advantages

  • High dynamics: High-speed motion of vehicles and pedestrians, simulating real-world emergencies
  • Multimodal data: Includes spike stream, RGB frames, optical flow ground truth, instance segmentation, and depth information
  • Extreme scenario coverage: Focuses on high-risk scenarios such as traffic accidents, filling the gap in real datasets
  • High temporal resolution: Spike stream generated at 40kHz, supporting microsecond-level optical flow analysis

RSSF Dataset

RSSF (Real Scenes with Spike and Flow) is a dataset contributed by the paper Learning Optical Flow from Continuous Spike Streams.

The goal of RSSF is to bridge the gap between synthetic data and real-world scenes, enhancing the generalization ability of models in real-world scenarios.

Data Source: Based on the high spatiotemporal resolution real-world dataset Slow Flow, which contains 41 scenes and tens of thousands of raw data frames.

Construction Process

  • Data Selection and Processing: 30 scenes from Slow Flow are selected as the training set, and 11 scenes as the test set. The raw data (Bayer format) is processed through ISP (demosaicing, white balance, intensity mapping) to generate color images, and downsampled by 2x in spatiotemporal resolution to save computational resources.
  • Optical Flow Ground Truth Generation: The GMA network (trained on mixed datasets Sintel, KITTI, HD1K, and FlyingThings) is used to generate reference optical flow for each frame.
  • Spike Stream Simulation: Based on the reference optical flow, scene motion is simulated to construct a virtual spike camera that generates spike streams. Images are interpolated with 20 time steps between each pair of adjacent frames, and precision is improved through 10x temporal oversampling. Optical flow ground truth covers motion at 20, 40, and 60 interpolated time steps (denoted as dt=20, 40, 60).

Key Features

  • Realism: Scenes are from the real world, avoiding the issues of unrealistic textures, lighting, and appearance in synthetic data.
  • High Precision Alignment: Spike streams and reference optical flow are generated through simulation, ensuring data correspondence.
  • Multiple Time Steps: Supports optical flow estimation tasks over different time spans.
